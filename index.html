✦   ✦   ✦
June 24, 2025

I stood in front of the shattered mirror, watching every piece resonating with my soul, feeling every crack of agony. My life, just like that, shattered in mere peices over a mistake.

What was my mistake? What did I do?

All I wanted was a smile, but instead, I was met with the same old cold gaze. Was it really this easy to break someone without caring? How selfish could you really be to behave like this?

1

I am 18 now. Old enough to understand who I am, and what I live for! I only yearned for an ounce of your affection for year.A hope that maybe one day you would be proud of me . But tonight after that fight about blood group , I decided to play along.

Tonight, I did the one thing you told me never to do. I went to the attic while you were out for grocery. I carefully took the keys in the way they were kept, memorising its position and unlocked the door.

2
June 25

I managed to do it. I waited until you left for your usual afternoon shift, and my heart was hammering so loud as if you could hear my thoughts and stop what i was about to do . I finally went up there.

The attic smelled like old dust and damp cardboard, and I knew I only had about 30 mins to get out. I started going through those plastic bins in the corner, the ones taped shut that you always told me were just “old tax papers.”

None of it was taxes.

3

I found a medical card from when I was five and snuck it back down to my room. Now here I’m staring at the blood type which makes me wonder what else you hid from me. It’s completely impossible for me to be your biological kid — at first I ignored it, as there were rare cases — but now what I held pointed otherwise.

Am I adopted… or worse… abducted?

When you got home last night and we sat down for dinner, I tried to bring up my medical history casually. You didn’t say a single word. You just gave me that same dead, cold look and completely changed the subject.

4
June 28

The isolation in this house is starting to feel less like “protection” and more like a massive cover-up.

I spent all morning looking around while you were out on your routine. Why are there literally zero photos of you pregnant with me? Why is my birth certificate a cheap photocopy issued only a few years ago with the hospital name blanked out?

5

Yesterday, I caught you whispering on the phone in the kitchen. You didn’t hear me come down the stairs. You said —

“She’s starting to ask questions. If she looks herself up online, we’re screwed.”

My mind is killing me with all these questions while you answer none. I am sure of that now.

6
July 2

I went back up there. I had to know the truth.

Tonight, while you were completely knocked out in bed, I took the keys again and unlocked a small metal lockbox buried at the bottom of the trunk. There were no adoption papers. No foster care records. Nothing.

Just a dusty envelope with a yellowed newspaper clipping inside — from exactly eighteen years ago.

↓  really??…

7
July 3

The headline on that old paper practically stopped my heart:

“Infant Abducted from City Hospital Nursery.”

My hands shook so much the page fell to the ground, as if mocking me. The date on it was June 15th. My exact birth month. I stared at the photo of these two strangers crying over their lost baby girl, and then my eyes dropped to the police sketch of the suspect.

8

It was your face. Your exact face from eighteen years ago.

I wasn’t adopted. I wasn’t some unwanted mistake. I was stolen.

The coldness I felt from you my entire life was not hatred — it was the paralyzing guilt of having to look at the kid whose life you stole every single day.

― ― ―

Suddenly, I felt your presence behind me. You saw the open box, the paper in my hands, and for the first time in eighteen years, your stone cold face completely cracked — a smirk downward.

9

You just quietly took a step back inside, shut the door, and turned the lock.

✦  And that's when i accepted my fate afterall  ✦
